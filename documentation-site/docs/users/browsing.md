# Browsing The Library

Prismedia has two complementary views of your library:

- **Browse pages** show catalog entities: videos, series, images, galleries, books, audio, people, studios, tags, and collections.
- **Files** shows watched roots and folders exactly as they sit on disk.

## Dashboard

The dashboard is the landing surface for recent media, library totals, worker state, and update notices.

![Dashboard](/img/screenshots/dashboard.png)

## Videos and series

The **Videos** page is the flat media view. It supports grid, list, and media-wall browsing, search, sort, filters, bulk actions, and quick navigation to detail pages.

![Videos](/img/screenshots/videos.png)

The **Series** page groups videos into series, seasons, and episodes based on folder layout. Series and season pages use the same detail shell and related-media grids as the rest of the app.

## Images and galleries

**Images** are loose image files. **Galleries** are folders or archive-like groups of images and animated media. Gallery details show child groups, image grids, metadata, ratings, tags, linked people, studios, and artwork.

![Galleries](/img/screenshots/galleries.png)

Clicking an image opens the universal lightbox with navigation, zoom, metadata, and linked-entity details.

## Books and comics

Books are scanned from `.cbz` and `.zip` archives. A standalone archive becomes a single book; archives in a folder can become chapters, volumes, and long-running comic or manga series.

The reader opens as a focused route with paged and vertical reading modes, resume state, chapter navigation, and mobile-friendly controls.

## Audio

Audio libraries are folder-backed groups of tracks. Library detail pages show sub-libraries, album-style track lists, cover art, ratings, markers, and linked metadata. Starting a track opens a persistent player that survives navigation.

![Audio](/img/screenshots/audio.png)

## People, studios, and tags

People, studios, and tags are library entities, not just labels. Their detail pages show associated videos, series, images, galleries, books, and audio based on explicit relationships.

![People](/img/screenshots/people.png)

## Collections

Collections are simple groupings for browsing and curation. They can contain videos, series, galleries, images, books, and audio tracks.

![Collections](/img/screenshots/collections.png)

Collection modes:

| Mode | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Manual** | You add, remove, and reorder items directly. |
| **Dynamic** | Rules select matching media. Refreshing the collection updates membership. |
| **Hybrid** | Manual pinned items plus rule-produced items. |

Collections are organizational views. They are not the app-wide playback queue.

## Files

The **Files** workspace is the file manager for watched roots.

![Files](/img/screenshots/files.png)

From Files you can:

- Open linked catalog entities.
- Create folders and upload files.
- Rename and move files or folders.
- Rescan a root, folder, or file.
- Exclude paths from future scans and remove exclusions later.
- Delete files or folders when the media mount is writable.

Use Browse when you want media metadata. Use Files when you want source-folder control.

## Search and command palette

The dedicated **Search** page spans every entity type and highlights direct matches first, followed by related media for matched people, studios, and tags.

![Search](/img/screenshots/search.png)

Press `Cmd+K` on macOS or `Ctrl+K` elsewhere to open the command palette from any page.

## Mobile

Mobile navigation keeps primary browse and operate surfaces reachable from touch-first layouts. Detail pages, grids, readers, lightboxes, Files, and audio playback all avoid hover-only core actions.
